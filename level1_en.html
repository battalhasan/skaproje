In the Shadow of the Trash Mountain

Under the May sun, the familiar but disturbing scent in the school's backyard was sharper than ever. Mert wiped the sweat from his forehead and put his phone in his pocket. His glasses had slipped down his nose; these new data points had him excited.

Mert: "Unbelievable! The app doesn't lie, Elif. The visual analysis results confirm that our weekly waste amount has nearly doubled compared to last year."

Mert handed the phone to Elif. On the screen, a graph of the container's contents was visible: 75% Organic, 15% Plastic, 10% Paper. Below it, a warning in red letters: "ESTIMATED AMOUNT: 120 KG."

After looking at the phone screen, Elif glanced at a half-eaten sandwich overflowing from the bin and an unopened ayran carton. She sighed.

Elif: "Data isn't just about numbers, Mert. What we're seeing here is the result of everyone who said, 'it won't matter just this once'."

Zeynep pointed to the crowd at the school entrance.
